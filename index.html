WHO Keys for Safer Food

AKANJI OLADAPO E. 245139

NUT 750 Assignment

Good food hygiene can prevent the transmission of pathogens responsible for many food borne diseases. Governments, industry and consumers have a shared responsibility in ensuring the safety of food. These are “Five keys to safer food”, which were developed to educate safe food handling behaviours to all consumers and food handlers.

Step 01.

Keep Clean

Regular hand washing and cleaning of all surfaces prevent contamination.

Step 02.

Separate raw and cooked

Avoid cross-contamination by keeping raw foods separate.

Step 03.

Cook thoroughly

Ensure food reaches safe temperatures to kill harmful bacteria.

Step 04.

Keep food at safe temperatures

Maintain proper temperature controls to slow bacterial growth.

Step 05.

Use safe water and raw materials

Use quality, fresh ingredients and safe water to prepare food.

Step 1: Keep Clean

Cleanliness is essential in preventing foodborne illnesses. Always wash your hands before handling food, after using the toilet, and after touching pets or raw food. Clean surfaces, utensils, and equipment thoroughly to avoid cross-contamination from harmful microorganisms that may be present in soil, water, or raw foods.

Step 2: Separate raw and cooked

Avoid cross-contamination by keeping raw foods, especially meat, poultry, and seafood, separate from cooked and ready-to-eat foods. Use separate cutting boards and utensils for raw and cooked items to prevent the transfer of harmful bacteria from raw to safe foods.

Step 3: Cook thoroughly

Proper cooking kills harmful microorganisms. Cook food to the appropriate temperature, especially meat, poultry, eggs, and seafood. Use a food thermometer when necessary, and make sure that soups and stews reach boiling point. Avoid partially cooked foods, especially when reheating.

Step 4: Keep food at safe temperatures

Bacteria multiply rapidly at room temperature. Keep hot foods hot (above 60°C) and cold foods cold (below 5°C). Do not leave cooked food at room temperature for more than two hours. Refrigerate promptly and thaw frozen food safely in the fridge, not on the counter.

Step 5: Use safe water and raw materials

Safe food preparation starts with clean water and quality ingredients. Use potable water for cooking and cleaning, and select fresh, wholesome foods. Wash fruits and vegetables thoroughly and avoid food past its expiry date or with signs of spoilage.
